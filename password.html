In this experiment, you were presented with a series of decision tasks involving the selection of blue or red pitchers, each containing varying proportions of colored balls.
Your decisions were made in a group setting, and the final choices were determined by majority votes.

Purpose of the Experiment and Research Hypothesis: The primary goal of this study was to investigate the strategic behavior of rational agents under conditions of uncertainty.
We aimed to understand how individuals make decisions when they have partial information and must rely on group majority to determine the outcome.
Specifically, we sought to examine how participants assess signal strength, group size, and the majority vote mechanism to choose between blue and red pitchers.

Strategic Behaviour of Rational Agents: In the experiment, you were asked to make choices that involved financial incentives, and your decisions were made strategically based on the available information.
This concept of strategic behavior by rational agents underlines the importance of making choices that maximize potential gains and minimize potential losses.

In situations characterized by uncertainty, individuals often employ strategies that take into account not only their private information but also the behavior of others.
This strategic interaction can lead to diverse decision patterns and outcomes, which we aimed to explore in this experiment.
